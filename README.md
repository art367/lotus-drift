## The Story

This wasn't designed in a studio or planned on a whiteboard. It was dreamed up in a single conversation — a vision of lotus flowers drifting across a moonlit pond, blooming at a human touch, synced to the rhythm of breathing.

The idea was simple: *what if a game could put you to sleep?*

Not by being boring. By being so gentle, so soft, so perfectly paced that your nervous system has no choice but to surrender. Every petal unfolds on the exhale. Every ripple slows the mind. Every firefly whispers *you're safe here.*

Built from nothing to live in one session. One HTML file. Zero dependencies. Pure meditation.

---

## What It Is

**Lotus Drift** is an interactive sleep experience disguised as a game. There are no scores, no timers, no enemies. Just a still pond at night, waiting for your touch.

- **Touch the water** → ripples spread, soft chimes ring
- **Touch a closed lotus** → it slowly blooms, releasing fireflies and light
- **Do nothing** → the world gently dims, guiding you toward sleep

The experience progresses through three invisible phases — from gentle activity to quiet drift to near-sleep — each one slower, dimmer, and more peaceful than the last.

## What's Inside

All 1,200 lines of a single `index.html`:

- 🎨 **Full canvas 2D rendering** — starfield, moonlight, water reflections, fog layers
- 🪷 **Organic lotus bloom system** — multi-layered petals with breathing-synced animation
- 🌊 **Ripple chain physics** — touch one flower and nearby ones react
- ✨ **Particle systems** — fireflies, light motes, drifting petals
- 🎵 **Generative audio engine** — pentatonic chimes, reverb, water ambience, drone
- 🧘 **Breathing synchronization** — bloom speed tracks an invisible breath cycle
- 🌙 **Progressive sleep phases** — the world naturally dims toward rest
- 📱 **Mobile + desktop** — touch and mouse, responsive canvas
